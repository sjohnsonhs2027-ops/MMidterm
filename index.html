Montefiore Square

About the Square

This community park in Harlem features an open lawn with terraced stone seating, a garden with perennial plants, and a paved plaza that can be used for art installations, markets, festivals, and performances.

Public Use

The plaza serves as a flexible civic space where residents can gather, relax, and participate in neighborhood events throughout the year.

Landscape Character

The site combines greenery, stone seating, and open circulation to create a calm but active urban space that welcomes both everyday use and larger public programming.

Site Highlights

Designed for gathering, movement, and neighborhood life

Open Lawn

A central green space provides room for rest, informal activity, and daily use in the heart of the plaza.

Terraced Stone Seating

Layered seating elements frame the lawn and support social use, observation, and small community gatherings.

Perennial Garden

Plantings soften the urban edges of the square and contribute seasonal color, biodiversity, and visual comfort.

Paved Plaza

The hardscape area supports art installations, local markets, cultural festivals, and outdoor performances.

Flexible Programming

The square is designed to adapt to different forms of public life, from quiet neighborhood use to larger organized events.

Neighborhood Identity

Montefiore Square acts as a community landmark and a welcoming public face for the surrounding Harlem neighborhood.

Community Stewardship

Local care and long-term advocacy

The Montefiore Park Neighborhood Association assists NYC Parks in maintaining this sloping triangle and plays a central role in the life of the space.

The Association played an important role in advocating for the 2021 park redesign, coordinates gardening, facilitates community involvement, and organizes events.

Why it matters

  • Supports long-term maintenance
  • Encourages neighborhood participation
  • Activates the plaza through public events
  • Strengthens local identity and stewardship
